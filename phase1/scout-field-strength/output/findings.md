**Key Finding**: The rate of change has increased since 2000, but this remains within historical norms.

### Context: Why This May Be Normal

1. **Natural Oscillation**: The field fluctuates in seemingly random patterns (heteroscedastic)
2. **Short Data Span**: 150-200 years of precise measurements is insufficient for geological projections
3. **Paleomagnetic Evidence**: Similar fluctuations observed throughout 3.4+ billion years of magnetic field history
4. **Reversal Precedents**: Field has reversed many times without complete collapse
